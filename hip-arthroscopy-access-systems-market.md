<p><p>Hip arthroscopy access systems refer to the tools and devices used during hip arthroscopic surgery, which is a minimally invasive procedure to examine and treat hip joint issues. There are two main types of access systems available in the market - Pincer Type and Cam Type. Pincer Type access systems are designed to address hip impingement caused by an overgrown socket, while Cam Type systems are used to address impingement caused by a misshapen femoral head. These access systems play a vital role in enabling surgeons to access and treat specific hip joint conditions effectively.</p></p>
