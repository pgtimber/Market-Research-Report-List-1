<p><p>Veterinary excipients can be classified into two main types: animal-based and plant-based. Animal-based excipients are derived from animal sources such as gelatin, glycerin, and stearic acid. These excipients are commonly used in pharmaceuticals and supplements for animals due to their stability and compatibility. On the other hand, plant-based excipients are derived from plant sources such as cellulose, guar gum, and corn starch. These excipients are preferred for their natural and sustainable properties, making them a popular choice in the veterinary industry.</p></p>
