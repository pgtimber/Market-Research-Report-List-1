<p><p>Liver function tests (LFTs) are a set of blood tests performed to assess the liver's overall health and its ability to function properly. These tests measure the levels of various enzymes, proteins, and other substances in the blood, which can indicate liver damage or disease. LFTs are often used to diagnose liver conditions such as hepatitis, cirrhosis, or fatty liver disease and to monitor their progression or response to treatment.</p><p>The global liver function tests market has been witnessing significant growth due to the increasing prevalence of liver diseases, such as hepatitis, liver cancer, and alcoholic liver diseases, worldwide. Rising awareness about the importance of early diagnosis and treatment of liver disorders is also fueling market growth. Moreover, the adoption of advanced technologies and the development of novel biomarkers for liver function testing are further propelling the market.</p><p>The market is also experiencing several latest trends that are contributing to its growth. <p><p>Liver Function Tests (LFTs) are crucial for diagnosing liver diseases and assessing liver health. They help measure enzyme levels and chemicals in the blood, indicating liver function. These tests include the Alanine Aminotransferase (ALT) and Aspartate Aminotransferase (AST) tests, which assess liver inflammation and damage. The Alkaline Phosphatase (ALP) test measures enzyme levels associated with bile duct health, while the Gamma GT (GGT) test detects liver and bile duct diseases. Total Bilirubin, Albumin, and other LFTs help evaluate liver function, providing essential information for accurate diagnosis and treatment of liver-related conditions.</p></p>
