<p><p>Hardware OTP token authentication refers to a security mechanism that generates unique One-Time Passwords (OTPs) for user authentication. There are different types of hardware tokens available in the market, including USB tokens, SIM tokens, and mini tokens. USB tokens are small devices that can be plugged into a computer's USB port to generate OTPs. SIM tokens, on the other hand, are integrated into a subscriber identity module card used in mobile devices for OTP generation. Mini tokens are compact hardware devices that generate OTPs, often used for portable and on-the-go authentication.</p></p>
