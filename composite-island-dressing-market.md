<p><p>Composite island dressings are advanced wound dressings that consist of multiple layers, combining the benefits of different materials to provide effective wound healing. These dressings typically include a non-adherent absorbent pad, an adhesive border, and a breathable film or foam backing. The non-adherent pad helps prevent adherence to the wound bed, while the adhesive border secures the dressing in place and protects the wound from external contaminants. The breathable backing promotes a moist wound environment and allows for better oxygen exchange.</p><p>The composite island dressing market is witnessing significant growth due to several factors. Firstly, the increasing prevalence of chronic wounds, such as diabetic foot ulcers, pressure ulcers, and venous leg ulcers, is driving the demand for advanced wound dressings. Secondly, the rising geriatric population and the subsequent increase in surgeries and wound-related complications are also contributing to market growth. Additionally, technological advancements in wound care products, such as the development of composite dressings with improved absorption and moisture retention properties, are further propelling market expansion.</p><p>Moreover, the COVID-19 pandemic has led to an increased focus on wound care and infection prevention. As a result, there has been a surge in demand for advanced wound dressings, including composite island dressings, to facilitate faster wound healing and reduce the risk of infection.
