<p><p>The Ocular Allergy Diagnostic Systems market includes two types of diagnostic methods: invasive diagnostics methods and non-invasive diagnostics methods. Invasive diagnostics methods involve procedures that require penetration of the skin or mucous membranes, such as skin tests or conjunctival allergen challenges. On the other hand, non-invasive diagnostics methods do not involve any penetration and are less discomforting for patients, such as tear film stability assessments or allergen-specific immunoglobulin tests. These different diagnostic methods offer healthcare professionals various approaches to diagnose and assess ocular allergies in patients.</p></p>
