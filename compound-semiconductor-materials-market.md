<p><p>Compound semiconductor materials can be categorized into three main types: III-Vs, II-VIs, and IV-IVs. III-V compounds, like gallium nitride and indium phosphide, are commonly used in optoelectronic devices. II-VI compounds, such as zinc selenide and cadmium telluride, are often used in high-speed electronics. IV-IV compounds like silicon carbide are popular in power electronics. Additionally, the sapphire market, which is primarily used for LED production, is also a significant segment in the compound semiconductor materials industry.</p></p>
