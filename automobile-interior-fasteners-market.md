<p><p>Automobile interior fasteners can be classified into two main types: Threaded fasteners and non-threaded fasteners. Threaded fasteners include screws, bolts, and nuts, which are designed to create a strong and secure connection by screwing into the material. On the other hand, non-threaded fasteners, such as clips, pins, and retainers, are used for quick and easy installation without the need for threading. Both types of fasteners play a crucial role in holding together various components of automobile interiors, ensuring durability and stability.</p></p>
