<p><p>The invasive brain monitoring device market comprises various types of devices used to monitor brain activity and measure neurological parameters. Electroencephalography (EEG) devices detect electrical activity in the brain, while magnetoencephalography (MEG) devices measure magnetic fields generated by brain activity. Transcranial Doppler (TCD) devices assess blood flow in the brain, and intracranial pressure (ICP) monitors measure pressure inside the skull. Cerebral oximeters monitor brain oxygenation, while magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) and computerized tomography (CT) devices provide detailed images of the brain. Positron emission tomography (PET) devices visualize brain function. Sleep monitoring devices assess sleep patterns, and electromyography (EMG) devices evaluate muscle activity.</p></p>
