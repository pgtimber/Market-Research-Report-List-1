<p><ul><li>Pre-exposure Prophylaxis</li><li>Post-exposure Prophylaxis</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Animal anti-rabies vaccines are used for both pre-exposure prophylaxis and post-exposure prophylaxis in animals. Pre-exposure prophylaxis involves administering the vaccine to prevent rabies infection before potential exposure, such as in pets or wildlife professionals. Post-exposure prophylaxis is given after a suspected rabies exposure to prevent the virus from infecting the animal. These vaccines play a crucial role in controlling and preventing the spread of rabies in animals, ultimately protecting both animals and humans from this deadly disease.</p></p>
