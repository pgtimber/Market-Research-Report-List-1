There are various types of market for gene knockdown, including siRNA and shRNA. Small interfering RNA (siRNA) molecules are synthetic double-stranded RNA that target and degrade specific mRNA, thereby silencing gene expression. Short hairpin RNA (shRNA) is a synthetic RNA molecule that is processed in cells into siRNA. Other markets related to gene knockdown may include antisense oligonucleotides and the use of viral vectors for gene silencing.</p></p>
