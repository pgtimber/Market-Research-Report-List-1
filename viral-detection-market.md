<p><p>The viral detection market encompasses various types of assays used to identify and quantify viruses in biological samples. Plaque assays involve the growth of viruses in cell cultures, forming visible plaques that can be counted and measured to determine viral concentration. Polymerase Chain Reaction (PCR) amplifies viral genetic material for detection, allowing for precise identification. Enzyme-Linked Immunosorbent Assays (ELISA) detect viral proteins or antibodies in samples using specific antibodies. Other viral detection methods include next-generation sequencing, flow cytometry, and immunofluorescence assays, all offering their own advantages in terms of sensitivity, specificity, and versatility.</p></p>
