<p><p>Monolithic ceramics can be classified into two main types: oxides and non-oxides. Oxides include ceramic materials such as alumina, zirconia, and silica, which are widely used for their excellent properties like high thermal stability and corrosion resistance. On the other hand, non-oxide ceramics like carbides, nitrides, and borides offer unique characteristics such as high hardness, thermal conductivity, and electrical insulation. Both types of monolithic ceramics play a significant role in various industries including electronics, aerospace, and automotive.</p></p>
