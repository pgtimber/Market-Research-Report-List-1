<p><p>Solid-liquid separation is a crucial process in downstream processing where various techniques are used to separate solids from liquids. Market types for solid-liquid separation include chromatography systems, filters, evaporators, centrifuges, dryers, and others. Chromatography systems separate compounds based on their molecular size, while filters physically trap solids. Evaporators remove liquid through evaporation, centrifuges use centrifugal force to separate solids, and dryers remove moisture from solids. Other techniques may include sedimentation, flocculation, and membrane filtration.</p></p>
