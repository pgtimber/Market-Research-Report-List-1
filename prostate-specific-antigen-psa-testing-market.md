<p><p>Prostate Specific Antigen (PSA) testing is commonly done using two main methods: Radioimmunoassay and Enzyme Immunoassay. Radioimmunoassay measures the levels of PSA in the blood by using radioactive isotopes to detect the antigen, while Enzyme Immunoassay uses enzymes to detect the antigen. Both methods are effective in diagnosing prostate cancer and monitoring treatment effectiveness. The market for these testing types is constantly evolving with advancements in technology and increasing demand for early detection of prostate cancer.</p></p>
